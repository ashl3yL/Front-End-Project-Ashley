Brooklyn Tech's Majors

The Majors of Brooklyn Tech

Brooklyn Tech has a total of 18 majors. During your sophomore year you'll see presentations regarding each major before you rank your major. Brooklyn Tech has you rank all 18 majors in a list before giving you your major based on your rank as well as your gpa in certain academic courses. At the end of your sophomore year The most popular majors in Brooklyn Tech are, Law & Society or LAS, Finance, and lastly Software Engineering. Brooklyn Tech's LIU Advanced Health Professions, and LIU PharmD are majors that will allow you to enter the neighboring college, Long Island University as a sophomore instead of a freshman.

Aerospace Engineering

Aerospace Engineering is all encompassing of everything with aircrafts, aviation, and even space. You'll not only be designing and creating aircrafts, but you'll also learn to fly.

Junior Courses

  • AP Physics 1 (Double-Period)
  • PLTW Principles of Engineering
    - PLTW stands for Project Lead The Way and can lead to a college credit through the Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT)

Senior Courses

  • Flight School (FAA Examination)
    - You'll be takeing the FAA Private Pilot's Knowledge Test. - The course ends with a simulated cross-country flight

Architecture

The Architecture Major is quite what it sounds like, where you learn about the planning, designing and constructing structures.

Junior Courses

  • Architectural Drawing
  • PLTW Civil Engineering & Architecture
    - Can lead to a college credit through Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT)
    - You use Rivet, a 3D design program from Autodesk

Senior Courses

  • Architectual Design/ Modeling
  • AutoCad with Certiport Certification
    - Students will learn how to use AutoCAD and take the AutoCAD certification exam.

Biological Science

Biological Science has you learn about the life sciences such as genetics, and biology. You;ll also have the chance to dissect organisms. The Bio Sci major is a relatively popular major partly because its the only major that has school trips.

Junior Courses

  • AP Biology (Double-Period)

Senior Courses

  • Genetics (One Semester | Double-Period)
  • Human Anatomy (One Semester | Double-Period)
  • AP Psychology
    - AP Psych is a very popular elective, another reason why Bio Sci is a popular major.

Chemistry

The Chemistry Major doesn't hide anything in its name. It's Chemistry. Chemistry majors take most of the courses required in the first two years of college chemistry. They also have opportunities for intermships and competitions.

Junior Courses

  • AP Chemistry (Double-Period)

Senior Courses

  • Organic Chemistry (One Semester | Double-Period)
  • Quantitative Analysis (One Semester | Double-Period)
  • Intro to Chemical Engineering (One Semester)
  • Moden Chemical Instrumental Analysis (MICA) (One Semester)

Civil Engineering

Civil Engineering focuses on the maintenance of structures whether they're man-made or environmental, from buildings to dams, to geology.
There are also many extended learning opportunities such as the American Society of Civil Engineers(ASCE), a student-run organization committed to provide information and resources to the Civil Engineering education. Work-based learning opportunities are also offered such as job shadowing and summer internships like the NYC School Construction Authority (SCA) and the NYC department of Design and Construction (DDC).

Junior Courses

  • Engineering Stactics
  • PLTW Civil Engineering & Architecture
    - Can lead to a college credit through Rochester Institute of Technology (RIT)
    - You use Rivet, a 3D design program from Autodesk
  • AP Physics 1 Optional

Senior Courses

  • American Concreate Institute Certification
  • Land Surveying Certification
  • AutoCad with Certiport Certification
    - Students will learn how to use AutoCAD, and will be introduced to SoftDesk-Civil, another program similar to AutoCAD but specifically for Civil Engineering problems.
    - Students will take the AutoCAD certification exam.
    - Students will have their own professional portfolio of their work for college.
